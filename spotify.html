1. Overview

A UX research and interface design project focused on enhancing Spotifyâ€™s mobile social experience. I co-led the project end-to-end, from initial concept to final prototype, exploring user needs around music sharing and designing new social analytics and messaging features, including a reimagined Jam experience to support connection and discovery.

2. Problem & Goal

While Spotify excels at individual listening experiences, its social features remain limited and unintuitiveâ€”particularly the Jam feature. Users lack integrated tools for in-app messaging, viewing follower analytics, or exploring music compatibility with friends.

This project aimed to:

  • Understand how users currently interact with Spotifyâ€™s social functions
  • Identify pain points and opportunities for sociability-focused feature growth
  • Explore how music spreads among friend groups, both on and off Spotify
  • Highlight the potential social value of music-centred interaction

3. Research

We began with a mixed-methods research approach to understand how users engage with Spotify socially, what gaps they experience, and what new features might enhance music-centred connection. This involved gathering both quantitative and qualitative data to identify key behaviors, needs, and pain points.

  • Competitor analysis of 5 direct and 3 indirect apps
  • Usability audit of Spotifyâ€™s existing social features
  • Survey (60+ responses) exploring social behaviors and feature gaps
  • 6 user interviews to dive deeper into user habits, preferences, and pain points
  • Graffiti wall ideation (2x) to gather rapid user impressions and feature desires

Key Insights

  • Music sharing often initiates and strengthens social bonds.
  • Users experience frustration with the current Jam feature's complexity and lack of interaction.
  • Many Spotify users rely on third-party apps to fill social interaction gaps.
  • Thereâ€™s a demand for real-time social features, like messaging and analytics sharing.
  • Spotify users want easier ways to connect with friends and discover music together.

4. Synthesis

After analyzing our research findings, we identified key patterns and user needs that would guide our design direction. These insights were synthesized into personas, journey maps, and affinity maps to highlight specific user pain points and areas for growth.

  • Created 2 user personas to represent key segments of Spotify users.
  • Developed user journey maps that illustrated common paths and friction points.
  • Used thematic network mapping to visualize and prioritise core user needs and feature requests.
  • Conducted an affinity mapping exercise to identify themes and key trends from qualitative data.

Need Statements

Based on the synthesis of our research, we formulated need statements that clearly define the core problems to solve for Spotify users. These statements served as a foundation for ideation and feature prioritisation.

  • Users need an intuitive way to share music with friends, without technical barriers, to foster social connection.
  • Spotify users need a seamless way to see and track their friendsâ€™ listening activity and music preferences.
  • Users need more personalized ways to discover new music through connections with others, both within and outside Spotify.
  • Users need a messaging feature within Spotify to communicate easily about shared music interests and recommendations.

5. Ideation & Prioritisation

With a solid understanding of user needs, we moved into the ideation phase. The goal was to identify and prioritise the most impactful features to address user pain points. Using a combination of creative ideation and strategic analysis, we narrowed down our ideas to those with the highest potential for user impact and feasibility.

  • Creative Matrix: We brainstormed multiple ideas and used the creative matrix to evaluate their potential based on user needs and technical feasibility.
  • Prioritisation Matrix: We assessed ideas by impact and effort, ensuring that the most valuable features were prioritised in our design process.
  • Product Definition: We outlined the key features of the product, focusing on social analytics, music sharing, and messaging integration.

6. Design

The design phase followed a two-pronged approach: redesigning the existing Jam feature and introducing new features that addressed the social interaction and discovery needs of Spotify users. We also incorporated in-app messaging to streamline interactions between users and enhance the overall social experience.

Design Outputs

Throughout this process, we produced wireframes, storyboards, and high-fidelity prototypes to visualize the user interface. Each iteration was validated with usability tests to ensure the designs aligned with user needs and expectations.

  • Wireframes: Low-fidelity wireframes were created to map out the layout for the Jam feature and the new features.
  • Prototypes: High-fidelity prototypes in Figma were developed for the Jam feature, Friends' Frequency, Local Listeners, and the messaging interface.
  • Usability Testing & Iteration: We conducted several rounds of usability testing to refine the interactions, ensuring ease of use and functionality.

Redesigning the Jam Feature

The Jam feature was identified as unintuitive and challenging for users. We redesigned it to improve ease of use and enhance its social functionality, ensuring a seamless experience for users when engaging with their friendsâ€™ music choices. The goal was to make the feature more intuitive and promote more spontaneous, in-app musical sharing.

  • Design Focus: Simplify the user interface and improve accessibility to increase user engagement and ease of sharing.
  • Testing & Iteration: The redesign went through multiple iterations based on usability testing feedback, which highlighted areas for more clarity and reduced cognitive load.

New Features

To enhance social interaction, we introduced two new features: Friends' Frequency and Local Listeners. These features were designed to help users discover and connect with others based on shared musical tastes and social data.

  • Friends' Frequency: This feature displayed mutual followersâ€™ top artists, songs, and playlists, allowing users to better understand their social music circles. It aimed to increase collaboration and discoverability among friends.
  • Local Listeners: Users could see compatibility scores with others within their city or geographic area, enabling music-based interactions and connections at a local level.
  • In-App Messaging: To improve communication between users, we integrated an in-app messaging feature that allowed users to easily share music and interact around shared interests.

7. Outcome

The redesign of the Jam feature, along with the introduction of the Friends' Frequency and Local Listeners features, significantly enhanced the social experience on Spotify. Users now have a more intuitive way to share music with friends, discover new artists based on mutual interests, and engage with the broader community through localized music discovery. In-app messaging further strengthened the connection between users, making interactions seamless.

Final Interface Concept

The final interface concept integrated all three key features in a way that was visually cohesive and functionally streamlined. The redesigned Jam feature made it easy for users to share their music moments, while Friends' Frequency and Local Listeners provided the social and discovery-driven functionalities that users had requested. The added in-app messaging feature created a seamless interaction flow.

Key Learnings

  • User-Centred Design: We learned the importance of continuously iterating on designs based on user feedback to ensure that features truly align with user needs.
  • Social Interaction Enhancements: Social features must feel integrated and natural within the app to ensure long-term engagement.
  • Testing is Crucial: Iterating through wireframes, prototypes, and usability tests helped us refine the final product and make the user experience as intuitive as possible.
