1. Overview

A UX research project focused on generating design recommendations for a user-centred mood-tracking app, emphasising customisability and flexibility to address gaps in personalisation found in existing apps. The final case study slides are embedded below, and the specific deliverables can be viewed at the Figma link below.

Included:
  • Competitor analysis (2 direct, 1 indirect)
  • Literature review
  • Survey (20+ responses)
  • User interviews (3x)
  • Thematic network mapping
  • User personas (2x)
  • User journey
  • Design recommendations
  • Case study presentation slides

2. Problem & Goal

Most mood-tracking apps offer limited flexibility, leading to low user engagement over time. Through this project, I set out to:

  • Investigate pain points and frustrations in existing mood-tracking experiences
  • Understand user attitudes toward social features and community sharing
  • Identify what keeps users motivated and consistent
  • Provide clear design recommendations based on qualitative and quantitative insights

3. Research

I took a mixed-methods approach to understand how users track their emotions, what challenges they face in doing so consistently, and what features could make mood tracking feel more intuitive, rewarding, and sustainable. This involved collecting both quantitative and qualitative data to surface user behaviors, needs, and blockers.

  • Competitor analysis of 2 direct and 1 indirect app
  • Literature review of current mood-tracking app trends and uses
  • Survey (20+ responses) tracking habits, motivation, and drop-off patterns
  • 3 user interviews to dive deeper into user habits, preferences, and pain points

Key Insights

  • Speed matters: 100% of users preferred mood tracking to take one minute or less. Time was the most cited challenge in both open-ended and scaled responses.
  • Customization is key: 86% wanted to customize how they represent moods, and preferred flexibility in tracking frequency. This reflected major gaps seen in competitor apps.
  • Insights drive motivation: All users selected "mood analysis and insights" as a top desired feature, often linking it to long-term personal growth.
  • Forgetfulness is a barrier: 60% of former users stopped tracking due to forgetting, and 77% rated reminders as highly useful.
  • Mixed views on social features: While over half were unlikely to connect with others on the app, a third expressed interest in features like following users or sharing mood streaks. Interview responses revealed more openness to these features than the survey data suggested.

4. Synthesis

I synthesised my research findings to identify core user needs, behaviors, and motivations that would shape my product strategy. Through persona creation, user journey mapping, and thematic analysis, I clarified where existing tools fall short and where new features could better support long-term engagement and user needs.

  • Created 2 user personas to reflect contrasting needs around routine-based vs. reflective mood tracking.
  • Developed a user journey map that illustrated a common path and friction points.
  • Used thematic network mapping to uncover major pain points like time burden, forgetfulness, and lack of personalisation.
  • Used survey and interview data to prioritise features based on user value and implementation feasibility.

5. Recommendations

Based on my research and synthesis, I developed a set of design recommendations to guide the next phase of ideation and prototyping. These focus on flexibility, meaningful insights, and optional social connection to support long-term user engagement.

Customisability as a Core Feature

  • Allow users to choose how and how often they track moods (e.g. once/day, multiple times/day, or flexible).
  • Offer multiple ways to represent moods (e.g. emojis, colors, sliders, text), reflecting the personal nature of emotional experiences.
  • Design a modular interface that supports personalization over time.

Insightful and Actionable Mood Analysis

  • Prioritize features that help users interpret mood data over time, not just visualize it.
  • Introduce light-touch recommendations or prompts based on trends (e.g. "You tend to feel more positive after journaling").
  • Highlight patterns to support emotional awareness and self-discovery.

Optional and User-Defined Community Layer

  • Consider introducing social features that are opt-in and user-controlled.
  • Explore formats like anonymous mood-sharing, community prompts (Reddit-style), or private friend-based tracking.
  • Provide toggles for visibility, notifications, and interaction level to ensure privacy and comfort.
